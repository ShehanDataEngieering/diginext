## 1. Swedish Tech Recruiter CV Review

**Date:** March 23, 2026
**URL:** https://claude.ai/chat/f607cbe1-b94a-42de-83b4-459d7d81cec1 / https://claude.ai/chat/6cf6ecf8-2427-408b-a6c2-4a2bda50f798

### Summary

Shehan asked Claude to roleplay as a Swedish tech recruiter and hiring manager for a JavaScript role to provide a strict critique of his CV. Two versions of this conversation exist (the second added market specificity).

### Recruiter Perspective (First 10 Seconds)

- **Location flag:** Gothenburg, not Stockholm
- **Work permit:** EU Residence Permit but needs sponsorship — flags candidate before reading
- **Gap:** Active commercial experience stops at mid-2023 (~3 years ago); masters gap + 5 months freelance not equivalent
- **Metrics:** "$500k to $600k monthly revenue boost" looks too polished — will be probed hard in screening
- **Swedish:** "Beginner via SFI" — quietly a no for most Swedish companies

### Hiring Manager Perspective

- Thesis metric "40% reduction (50 to 30ms)" — not a significant engineering achievement, just normal optimization
- Multiple technologies listed but only one job each — no way to assess depth vs exposure
- Freelance = one client, 5 months, not production scale
- Missing GitHub link credibility check
- **Combined verdict:** 6.5/10 in Sweden, **7.5/10 in Poland/Estonia/Portugal**

### Second Review (Strict but Constructive Lens)

Flags added in the second session:

- `"Available from July 2026"` — most hiring pipelines run 4–8 weeks, so this is a skip for active headcount
- `"Eligible to work across Europe with work permit"` — contradictory with EU Residence Permit, confusing to recruiters
- Gap (Aug 2023 – Oct 2025) not explained in summary; recruiter shouldn't have to hunt for it
- `"Returning to industry immediately upon graduation"` — 9 months post-graduation, now works against him
- Thesis blurb parenthetical reads defensive — drop it
- Single project in Key Projects looks thin

### Outcome

CV updated in both PDF and Word format with all fixes applied. Conclusion: **No in Sweden for cold applications.** Three silent filters fire before recruiter reads properly.
